*I used to have a blog*.

Back when I returned from my study exchange in Vienna and was wrapping up my
master's thesis in Brno, I wrote weekly about my life. That carried over all
the way into my time of living in Vienna more permanently, doing a PhD at
TU Wien and enjoying the new life abroad. 

That blog doesn't exist anymore. I stopped writing regularly during Covid, and
at some point took the website down to use the domain for my academic profile.
But I do miss it. I wonder what kind of memories I could have captured between
those times and now. My life is significantly different: got married, got a
kid, live in the US.

So here I am, setting up this new reincarnation. It might be useful to put down
some reason for why to write a blog:

- I want to practice formulating ideas and writing about what I work on
publicly. I believe it's easy to think you'd be good at this once the
opportunity comes, but in practice it takes a real skill. I want to get better
at writing in my voice, without an LLM. I do a lot of writing for scientific
publications, but that's a very different type of writing.
- To document a part of our lives. Next year will likely be a crucial breaking
point in terms of my career and where we end up living. Should be interesting
to capture.
- To own my content. If I was optimizing for reach, I should probably post to
Instagram or similar. But that's not what we're doing here.

A non-exhaustive list of people whose blogs I'm currently inspired by: [Tom
Macwright](https://macwright.com), [Cassidy
Williams](https://cassidoo.co/blog/), [Mitchell
Hashimoto](https://mitchellh.com/writing), and of course, my good friend
[Trevor Manz](https://trevorma.nz/blog).

At this point, I have only a rough idea for the content. It's probably going to
be less technical, I want to write a bit about my life, but I would also love
to use this space to write more about (side) projects that I'm working on.
