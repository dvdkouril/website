This really was supposed to be three separate blogposts, one for each month, but
as each month's last days approached, I just couldn't bring myself to finish
anything. 

Long story short: we're currently in my least favorite time of the year.  Every
year I promise that I'll romanticize the winter, enjoy the cosiness.  But when
it comes, it is still dreadful to be home most of the time, unless we want to
layer up to spend a bit of time outside in the cold. It's not worth the effort.
I keep thinking about how much different our life is in winter compared to
summer. Last summer, for example, we could spend like 3 or 4 hours on one of our
bike adventures with Nina.

![test](/blog-images/test1.jpg)

At least it got interesting by having a good amount of snow! I barely remember
when was the last winter when I experienced this much snow. There were two
distinct snowstorms that I remember, with weather advisory and all.

I barely remember anything from February, because we got completely wiped out by
some seasonal sickness. In the whole month, there's been just about three (3!)
days where everybody in my family was healthy. Not fun at all.

Another factor contributing to a regular seasonal depression is a yearly
deadline that we have in the visualization community: the IEEE VIS conference
full papers deadline. I've never liked working on submissions to this
conference, the timing aligns with general end of winter exhaustion, and the
actual final cut off time used to fall onto 2am in the European timezone. That
basically implies working into the night, until the last minute. The past years
they've changed the cutoff to be midnight Anywhere on Earth, which is even
worse, at least anywhere I've ever lived and worked.

I was going to try to put together a submission this year, but eventually
decided against it. I lost a bunch of time in February, either being sick myself
or having less capacity to work while taking care of another family member. And
when I got sick again the week before the deadline, I called it off. I'll make it
a journal submission, I guess.

The VIS deadline is typically such a landmark for each year, it almost splits
the year in half, even though it doesn't really make sense proportionally. Last
year, we flew back to Czech Republic in mid April to visit family and have a
little vacation. Now that it had ended (for me earlier than expected), it feels
a bit weird to look beyond March. 

But of course, it wasn't all bad. In January, I got a notification for a paper
accepted to a journal (Bioinformatics). I also worked on a couple of smaller
side projects:
- last year's exercising visualization
- scattered
- linked views blogpost

I also baked a bunch of bread. Took me a while to get a sourdough starter, well,
started. We have a really cold and drafty apartment and I naively used just a
regular bread flour. Once I started adding rye flour, the activity exploded. Of
course, I'm still figuring stuff out, as it turns out, there's a lot of
variables and a lot to learn to bake consistently okay-ish bread. As a side
effect, however, whenever I now have a regular toast bread, it just smells
weird.

And for the final topic: the future. My postdoc contract is ending in June
and I've decided that this is likely it for my time in academia. This past year
was really stressful, with all the funding uncertainty, insecurity around
contract extensions, and consequentially also our visas. Even though the
situation has somewhat stabilized now, and I could maybe stick around for
another year---or at least a couple of months---I think after three years it's
time to figure out a long-term solution.

I'm still open to any and all options and have been doing some semi-serious job
searching recently. So for the next three (remaining) months, my plan is the
following:
- be less sick, hopefully
- do some serious **job hunting**
- get back into a rhythm of **exercising**: jiujitsu, kettlebells, calisthenics,
maybe even some running
- enjoy the **spring in Cambridge**, meet with friends, go on bike rides

The year of 2026 should be an interesting one. It's one of the motivating
factors for starting to write a blog again: I want to have it documented. 

Let's see what happens.
